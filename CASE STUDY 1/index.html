I. BACKGROUND

What do I want to do?

Upon receiving this assingment to redesign a government agency website, I realized something... I have never been to a government agency website. Upon browsing the various government sites it was clear that this was going to be a difficult project, with tons of information and different heirarchies of program offices under the same umbrella all vying for space on a single website. I decided to use this opportunity to focus on the information architecture and reconfigure the navigation to reach deeper levels of the site with fewer clicks and less confusion. I also wanted to hone in on my skills in Figma to create a more enticing visual brand experience.

Who am I doing it for?

My first struggle came when trying to identify who would use a government agency website, and in particular what type of information is available on the site? What are the potential use cases I would be dealing with? Who is their target audience? What is the purpose of the Department of Enegy website? All these questions needed to be aswered, so I decided to create a proto-persona based off of my assumptions to get an idea of what a potential user might look like.

II. USER RESEARCH

Proto Persona

Michael
49 years old
Springfield, Illinois
High School Teacher
Married, 2 kids

Michael visits energy.gov to stay updated on new policies, new energy technology and anything energy related that may affect the way his family lives in the US. It’s important to Michael that he receives clear information about the way energy is being used. He also wants to own a solar roof someday so he has been looking for articles to read.

As a science teacher, he wants to find resources he can use in the classroom to teach his STEM students.

Michael is also looking for ways to modify his home to make it more energy efficient. He wants to feel a sense of pride for doing his part, and he feels that it is time his home got an upgrade. He is interested in DIY projects but doesn’t know where to start.

  • BIO:

    Michael is happy in his forever home in Springfield. His two children are attending the same high school he teaches science at. As a science teacher he wants to express the importance of taking care of the environment. He knows the science behind why it matters, but is unsure exactly how to carry over this into his real life. He recycles, picks up garbage around his neighborhood, and has a compost pile at home but wants to lower his energy bill inside his home as well. Solar power seems too expensive, but he dreams of having a solar roof someday.

  • FRUSTRATIONS:

    - Thinks that children should be better educated about the global impacts of energy consumption.
    - Having to dig through information to find what he needs.
    - Not being given step-by-step instructions when doing projects.
    - Doesn’t want to feel pressured into making purchases, or guilted into ‘saving the environment’.

  • MOTIVATIONS:

    - Happy in his home, even though it is an older house.
    - Wants to set a good example for his children by taking care of the environment.
    - Wants to save money on his energy bill.
    - Loves solar power.
    - Enjoys doing home improvement projects, but hires out for larger tasks.
    - Loves to browse websites for teaching resources he can use in the classroom.

User Testing

To understand users painpoints and frustrations with using the site we decided to outline a user flow, which would be the steps that we think Michael might go through to reach some of his goals as a potential user so we could test it on real people. The tasks defined by Michaels goals are as follows...

Tasks

1. View any news updates and find an article about solar roofing.
2. Find a DIY project that gives you step by step instructions.
3. Find a link to resources for teachers of high school level STEM students.

Results

During our user tests we discovered that most users felt like they would come to this site for a particular purpose, so making the search bar easier to identify would be helpful.
We also discovered that the navigation system on the current website is confusing, especially as you can see in the video above ^ when the user clicks into a particular section, an entirely new dropdown menu system gets put into place which left the users feeling uneasy and uncertain.
